    7 Facts About Tennis:

    #1The word “Tennis” comes from the Anglo-Norman term “Tenez.”

    #2The longest tennis match took 11 hours and 5 minutes to complete. It was played between John Isner and Nicolas Mahut. Ultimately, John Isner triumphed with a score line of 6–4, 3–6, 6–7, (7–9), 7–6(7–3), 70–68 (final set).

    #3 Don Budge is the only male player in tennis history to have won six consecutive Grand Slam singles titles, from Wimbledon in 1937 to the US Open in 1938.

    #4 In 2007, the prize money for Wimbledon winners became equal for men and women.

    #5 The term “Love” used in the scoring system of tennis is said to have originated from the French word for “egg,” l’oeuf, because a zero on a scoreboard resembles an egg. However, these claims are unsubstantiated.

    #6 Did you know that if the ball hits a player’s body or any part of their clothing before it lands, it is their opponents point (even if it would have gone out)?

    #7 Tennis balls were originally white. In 1986, yellow balls were first introduced at Wimbledon.
